# Framework Compendium (substantive summaries)

A reference artifact for working memory. Contains substantive summaries (1-3 sentences) of every claim, principle, and structural element. No proof structure, no citations, no examples — enough to grok the substance, not the support.

For full content, see `Working_Learning_Framework_v0_2.md` in this directory.

**Note on scope:** this compendium captures stable framework beliefs only. Transient working memory (active audits, deferred items, observed patterns) is kept in the author's private notes and is not part of the published repository.

**Convention: [implies]** — this marker flags content that is derived from the framework as an implication rather than stated literally in the framework document. The compendium's purpose is to help summarize both explicit claims and load-bearing implicit claims; the marker separates the two so I don't accidentally treat implications as if they were stated framework content. **Cap: two `[implies]` per claim.** When a third would be added, options are: drop a less-useful existing implies, merge multiple implies into one tighter formulation, or recognize the new content is substantive enough to graduate to framework proper.

**Convention: claim titles are verbatim.** Each claim's **bold title** matches its title in the full framework document character-for-character (the title is the stable identifier that joins the two documents). Only the *body* is compressed here. The light `[type]` tags after compendium titles (e.g. `[execution]`) and the `→ operationalizes...` pointers are not part of the title; derivation type, operationalization mapping, citations, and traceability all live in the full text — consult Working_Learning_Framework_v0_2.md if a session needs them.

This compendium is regenerated periodically as the framework evolves. ## Framework structure

- **Section 0**: Stance (executive summary, written last, dumping ground active)
- **Section 1**: Historical and epistemic notes (executive summary, written last, dumping ground active)
- **Future architecture**: T.A.P.E. (Theory → Practice → {Application, Engineering}); A and E layers deferred
- **Part I**: Theory (T-claims describing cognitive phenomena)
- **Part II**: Practice (P-claims operationalizing T-claims for parent decisions)
- **Vocabulary**: substrate (cognitive precondition; T-layer; not directly observable), capability (observable performance under specific conditions; P-layer), readiness (parent's judgment that learner has capability to attempt something new)

## Section 0 stance notes (consolidated, dumping ground)

1. **Measurement-disturbs-state as epistemic foundation** — Perfect a priori evaluation is incoherent because measurement reshapes the substrate it tries to measure. By the time evaluation completes, the learner is no longer the learner the evaluation began on. P10 (trait priors), P1 (flexibility), P9 (binning-as-engineering), and principled optimism all derive from this root.

2. **Data-king discipline** — Seek out places with data; evaluate contested points based on data. Simple methodological commitment. Produces preference for empirical studies over theoretical-only papers when adjudicating disputes.

3. **Measurement-naming corrupts what can be measured** — The act of naming a measurement target already corrupts observation, before any decision to optimize. Naming shapes attention, behavior, and situation-design automatically. Only double-blind study escapes this; everything else has corruption proportional to how much naming shapes attention. Goodhart's Law is the special case where this corruption is amplified by optimization. Framework cannot claim clean measurement of itself; only measurement with known corruption.

4. **Honest disclosure of framework's own data limits** — Framework originally started with one parent's informal observation while tutoring plus folk theory from self-help books; doctorate-trained author then audited claims against literature. Surviving claims tagged [literature] (literature-backed) or [synthesis] (reasoning from literature without direct empirical evidence). Defense is the audit discipline and cited literature, not observational origin. Active deployment-and-validation observation has not yet begun.

5. **Stance toward field debates** — Most debates are matters of degree not binaries. Framework specifies conditions rather than picking sides. Departures only when AI engineering structurally requires AND damning empirical evidence AND alternate framing explains apparent successes (VAK matching is the clearest departure case).

6. **Principled optimism about constraint malleability** — Default to "constraints are more malleable than they appear" rather than "constraints are fixed." Grounded in asymmetric error costs (cost of trying-and-failing is small; cost of not-trying-when-it-would-have-worked is large). Empirically supported by Mexican mouse study and similar findings.

## Section 1 stance notes (consolidated, dumping ground)

1. **T/P separation as structural answer to DDF/CLT conflict** — Desirable difficulties and cognitive load theory appear opposed only when treated as competing prescriptions. T-claims describe cognitive phenomena (T10 captures CLT-tradition expertise reversal; T5/T7 capture DDF-tradition variation and retrieval); P-claims operationalize each tradition under specific conditions. The apparent conflict dissolves when theory-claims and practice-prescriptions are kept distinct.

2. **Field positioning** — Closest theoretical fit cognitive science generally; closest applied fit AIED (BlueSky track for binning paper); EdPsych (Bjork/Sweller traditions) is the field framework most engages with; CLA differs architecturally; framework operates at the boundaries of these fields rather than being native to any.

## T-claims (Theory)

- **T1. Learning trajectories are non-monotonic** — Apparent state varies session-to-session in ways that aren't noise. Trajectories show U-shaped patterns, regressions, and plateaus reflecting reorganization rather than measurement variability. Continuous-cognition view (Siegler's overlapping waves) is the underlying reality; staircase models are decision-support binning at the practice layer. [implies] any fixed staircase model or category model is most likely an engineering decision-support tool which may have historical ties to being labeled truth while scientists were still working things out.

- **T2. Cognitive measurement is bounded** — All cognitive measurement disturbs the state being measured (Webb et al. 1966 empirically established this by comparing obtrusive and unobtrusive measurement). The state includes not just information/skill but the learner's beliefs about themselves and the task. Disturbance can be reduced through unobtrusive measurement (physiological proxies, naturalistic observation, implicit tests, continuous monitoring) but with trade-offs: lower disturbance generally means measuring correlates rather than the target directly. Three further bounds: practice effects on retest, multidimensionality of competence projected onto single numbers, and the epistemic distinction between psychometric measurement and classroom assessment. How to measure unobtrusively is outside the scope of this framework.

- **T3. Success in narrowed conditions is not evidence of capability** — Practice in narrowed conditions (heavy scaffolding, repeated single format, decontextualized drill) produces representations bound to those conditions. Embedded learning under diverse-input pressure produces representations connecting more broadly to cognition. Grounded in the learning-vs-performance distinction (Soderstrom & Bjork 2015): behavioral output under narrowed conditions can be high-performance without being durable learning; the discriminator is whether output survives changed conditions. Two mechanisms support the brittleness prediction: the unified-representation effect (diverse-input training connects more broadly to cognition) and decay vulnerability (T6, isolated skills decay faster lacking ambient anchors).
  - **T3a. Scaffolding-bound brittleness** — A learner who produces correct outputs only when prompted through each step is consistent with either acquisition or non-acquisition; the prompted output does not discriminate. Increasing scaffolding narrows the context toward engineered isolation along the active-execution dimension. Discriminator: delayed independent performance — if outputs survive prompt removal (with delay), the representation extended beyond the scaffold; if they collapse, it was scaffold-bound.
  - **T3b. Format-bound brittleness** — A learner who produces correct outputs across sessions in the same format may have a verbal/procedural performance reflecting no underlying schema. Unlike scaffolding-bound brittleness (which self-corrects when prompts are removed), format-bound brittleness can persist indefinitely and resists detection by assessment mirroring the training format. Discriminator: performance under surface-form variation — if outputs survive perturbation, schema is present; if they collapse, more practice in the original format is contraindicated (deepens the format-bound representation).

- **T4. Some skills require explicit instruction; they are not acquired through ambient encounter alone** — For a small class of skills, no amount of ambient encounter (however rich, active or passive) builds the skill; it must be constructed through deliberate explicit instruction, after which ambient encounter can maintain it. Two evidenced members: systematic decoding (Ehri et al. 2001, NRP meta-analysis d = 0.41) and arithmetic-fact automaticity (McNeil et al. 2025). For decoding, rich-text immersion without explicit letter-sound instruction produces context-guessing that resembles reading until context stops carrying the load; for arithmetic, ambient activity builds number sense but not automaticity (retrieval practice builds that; understanding and retrieval are complementary, not substitutes). Ambient activity remains valuable as scaffolding but does not build the skill. Deliberately narrow and extensional: operates on skill components not whole subjects (decoding ≠ all of reading; arithmetic facts ≠ all of math), class membership established empirically per-skill, framework takes no position on why the class exists and makes no attempt to predict membership. Orthogonal to T7 (confirmed by falsifiability test: neither claim's truth bears on the other's).

- **T5. Transfer is real where schemas overlap and the learner recognizes it** — Transfer is real where two conditions both hold: the overlap between contexts is real (shared components, not mere abstract resemblance) and the learner recognizes it. Accepts the identical-elements tradition (Singley & Anderson 1989, text-editor transfer) and analogical-transfer tradition (Gick & Holyoak), rejecting only far transfer (RT5). Recognition is the gate, not the existence of shared structure: Gick & Holyoak's ~30% spontaneous vs. ~75% cued solution rates show the shared structure was available the whole time; recognition determined use. Regardless of the exact mechanism (reconstruction vs. direct use), breadth of knowledge provides more material for new learning (Bransford & Schwartz 1999, preparation for future learning). T5 is mechanism-agnostic by design — it states transfer is real and bounded by overlap + recognition, without committing to a memory mechanism. Generality emerges from triangulation across at least two instances.

  - **T5a. Deriving facts new to oneself requires suspending plausibility checking** [literature, synthesis] — Personal innovation (term coined for this framework) is a person deriving new facts about the world through their own knowledge; such facts may or may not be novel in the general sense. Mechanism: imagine the possible with no plausibility filter, then filter to eliminate nonsense, then validate surviving links with an external source (teacher, book, or — if no expert exists to consult — experiment), because the feeling of understanding a self-generated conclusion systematically outruns actual understanding (illusion of explanatory depth, Rozenblit & Keil 2002, strongest for exactly this causal-explanatory kind of knowledge). Applying the plausibility filter during generation has two costs — it suppresses candidates that would otherwise surface (Amabile 1979; Amabile, Goldfarb & Brackfield 1990) and degrades recognition of the good candidates among those that do (Mueller, Melwani & Goncalo 2012). Once candidates are surfaced, the near-transfer literature takes over (T5); the only difference is the bridge was self-made rather than supplied. Distinct from general innovation (nobody knew it): general = personal innovation + verification-and-documentation apparatus (literature search, quantification, publication/use); doctorate and business each teach a version of that apparatus, but the added effort is verification, not generation. Elevating personal to general innovation is out of scope (a different bar: truth/value for others). Provenance: the personal/general boundary is grounded in first-hand dual-track experience (doctorate + patents), not literature inference.

- **RT5. Far transfer is rejected** — Far transfer (learning in domain A producing benefit in incompatible domain B via abstract shared base) fails replication consistently. Two domains may share abstract structure, but until the learner builds a bridging language between them, the shared structure is inaccessible. (Near transfer is now T5's positive claim — real where overlap exists and is recognized.) Narrow far-transfer successes accommodated by reconstruction via accumulated bridging language; no separate mechanism needed. Anderson, Reder & Simon (1996) critique engaged: what they call transfer via abstract representations is sub-skill reuse from accumulated repertoire — a case T5 already covers, with T5 adding the construction-in-target-domain component ARS leave implicit.

- **T6. Skills decay through disuse; spacing and active recall counter decay** — Acquired skills decay over time without retrieval (effect sizes up to d = -1.4 after 365 days). Memory has separable retrieval strength (erodes through disuse) and storage strength (persists longer). Ambient skills (numerical reasoning, literacy) decay slowly because the world supplies recall occasions; non-ambient skills decay quickly once instruction stops.

- **T7. Encounter type affects memory: active production exceeds passive exposure** — Active production (retrieval practice, active ambient encounter) substantially outperforms passive exposure (restudy, passive ambient encounter) for retention. Distinct from decay dynamics (T6). Ambient skills predominantly use active ambient encounter across heterogeneous surface forms; explicit skills exist primarily within curricular contexts and require deliberate retrieval scheduling.
  - **T7a. Passive observation and mental practice produce partial but distinct cognitive effects** — Passive observation builds the recognition/perceptual layer; mental practice builds part of the cognitive-strategy layer. Neither produces the predictive model that drives execution under uncertainty in dynamic conditions — that requires active prediction-and-consequence loops. Both are real cognitive products that accelerate later active practice (and can partially substitute when active practice is impossible), so prior observation or mental rehearsal yields faster active-practice acquisition because a layer is already built when active practice begins.

- **T9. Motivation affects learning** — All things being equal, positive motivation increases learning rate; negative motivation decreases it. Empirical grounding from multiple traditions (achievement goal orientation, self-determination theory, self-efficacy); framework doesn't commit to specific theory at T-level. Effect sizes modest (r ≈ 0.10 to 0.60) but reliable.
  - **T9b. Learning experience affects future motivation** — Pekrun's control-value theory: achievement emotions during an activity feed forward into motivation for re-engagement. Camacho-Morles 2021 meta-analysis (68 studies): enjoyment ↔ performance ρ = 0.27; anger ↔ performance ρ = -0.35; boredom ↔ performance ρ = -0.25. Talsma 2018 cross-lagged: performance → self-efficacy β = 0.205 (~3× the reverse direction). Mastery experience — actual performance success — is the strongest single predictor of self-efficacy in the literature (Britner & Pajares 2006; Usher & Pajares 2008). Combined with T9, completes feedback loop: motivation affects learning; learning experience affects subsequent motivation, with the learning → motivation direction being substantially stronger.
  - **T9c. Belief-installation interventions produce smaller effects than motivation-learning correlations suggest** — Belief-based motivation interventions produce real but small effects, smaller than T9 correlations would suggest. Growth mindset d = 0.03-0.10 (Sisk 2018, 273 studies; Yeager 2019, NSLM n=12,490). Grit r = 0.18 (Credé 2017, n=66,807), largely overlapping with conscientiousness. Self-efficacy installed in isolation: β = 0.071 (Talsma 2018 cross-lagged panel — the methodologically cleanest estimate, controlling for reciprocal structure). Motivation correlates with learning at modest magnitudes; installing motivation produces smaller effects than even those correlations suggest. Talsma effect is age-moderated: for children specifically, β ≈ 0 — installed self-efficacy does not measurably impact subsequent performance; only performance experiences build self-efficacy. Theoretical grounding: self-efficacy intervention requires metacognitive reflection capacity (Flavell 1979) that children have not yet developed.

- **T10. Substrate is precondition for acquisition** — A learner without prerequisite vocabulary or conceptual repertoire to attach a new schema cannot acquire it regardless of practice volume; practice in that condition deposits performance without acquisition. The expertise reversal effect (Tetzlaff 2025: d = 0.505 for low PK + high-assistance, d = -0.428 for high PK + low-assistance) provides empirical evidence via contrasts at extremes; the underlying phenomenon is continuous. Chen, Kalyuga & Sweller 2017 reframe as a special case of element interactivity: the same content imposes different cognitive loads at different prior knowledge levels because prior schemas chunk content into element groups. Extremes inform the continuous gradient, not discrete states. Absence of substrate is a contraindication for the activity, not something further instruction overcomes in the current session.

- **T13. Biological substrate and accumulated environment interact dynamically across development** — Genetic influences on cognition appear stronger or weaker depending on environment. Wilson Effect: heritability increases with age (~41% early childhood → 55% adolescence → 66-80% adulthood) as people increasingly select environments matching their predispositions. Mexican mouse study: heritability dropped from 55% to 15% under enriched environments. What looks like biological ceiling is often current-conditions ceiling.

- **T14. Teaching a subject is a different but related knowledge type than the subject itself** — Knowing a subject is one knowledge type; knowing how to teach the subject is another. Doctorate-without-industry pattern: practitioners can have deep subject knowledge without teaching capability. PCK requires partial decomposition of integrated knowledge for transmission.

- **T15. Procedural knowledge becomes integrated through accumulated practice, shifting from effortful to automatic execution** — Procedures become fluent and eventually automatized through practice. Fitts-Posner stages: cognitive (effortful, conscious) → associative (refining) → autonomous (integrated, no longer requiring conscious access). ACT-R compilation supplies the mechanism. The integrated/autonomous state is what stage 4 binning operationalizes.
- **T16. Academic burnout is a depleted state with measurable effects on learning** — Burnout (Maslach three-factor: emotional exhaustion, cynicism, reduced sense of accomplishment) negatively affects achievement (Madigan & Curran 2021 meta-analysis: r = -0.24 across 29 studies, N = 109,396; reduced-efficacy r = -0.39). Causal model: effort-reward imbalance (Siegrist 1996). Burnout is depleted state, not just disengagement; meta-analyzed interventions (mindfulness, CBT, REBT) produce g = 0.90 reduction (Madigan, Kim & Glandorf 2023, 17 studies).

- **T17. Declarative memory recall is reconstruction, not retrieval** — Declarative memory (facts and events that can be consciously stated) is reconstructed at recall, not played back from storage (Bartlett 1932). Fallibility is a defining property: leading questions reshape witness reports (Loftus & Palmer 1974); entire events can be implanted as confident memories (Loftus & Pickrell 1995). Scoped to declarative memory only — non-declarative memory (procedural, conditioning, priming) is a dissociable system the framework does not claim is reconstructive and does not adjudicate. No mechanism claimed (how/why not answered, not load-bearing). Load-bearing claim is narrow: wherever declarative recall enters learning, it enters as reconstruction, not playback.

- **Implication of holding T5 and T17 together** — Once all declarative recall is reconstruction (T17), "transfer is real" (T5) can only mean that reconstruction *capacity* is real and improves with breadth — there is no stored schema that ports, so what improves is the ability to reconstruct using accumulated prior material. The reusable reconstruction machinery this implies need not be named or its mechanism specified; it follows from holding both claims. T5 and T17 are independently defensible (one can hold either without the other), so they remain orthogonal; this is an emergent implication, not a dependency.

## P-claims (Practice)

- **P1. Flexibility in real time** [execution] — Rigid execution of fixed plans is strictly inferior to flexible execution that responds to learner state. Flexibility includes adjusting help-amount and recognizing when to abandon a plan that isn't working. Operates as the structural answer to the irreducible inaccuracy of starting priors.
  - **P1a. Ignoring learner frustration prevents learning** [execution, motivation] — A fixed plan that doesn't respond to rising frustration causes the session to fail. Frustration occupies working memory, displaces engagement, and feeds forward into reduced motivation for the next encounter (T9b). Camacho-Morles 2021: anger ↔ performance ρ = -0.35, boredom ↔ performance ρ = -0.25. Response options range from light (adjusting pace or difficulty) to heavy (abandon plan); ignoring is not on the menu.

- **P2. Warmup** [execution] — First couple of problems should be easier than the targeted activity. Provides a low-cost re-entry to the skill, manages cognitive startup cost, and gives the parent quick observation of current state before harder material.

- **P3. Test-taking is a skill that must be learned** [planning] — Test-taking conditions (timing, withheld scaffolding, distilled information, feedback at giver's preference) are sufficiently different from practical experience that test-taking warrants treatment as its own skill cluster. The framework's interactive teaching mode doesn't build these sub-skills, so periodic practice does. DI is mentioned as a methodology with strong evidence specifically for this cluster.

- **P4. Surface-form perturbation** [judgment] — When outputs appear correct in familiar form, vary the surface form to test whether schema generalizes. Outputs surviving variation indicate abstraction robust to that variation; outputs collapsing indicate format-bound brittleness (T3b). Repeating in original form deepens brittleness rather than building schema.
  - **P4a. Use three different high-element-interactivity contexts to verify generalization** [judgment] — Engineering-heuristic inversion: where engineering plans for three iterations to build, learning uses three to verify. Triangulation logic: one instance has contingent details that look structural; two instances can't distinguish contingent from structural; three instances let them separate. Each context must involve active prediction-and-consequence, not passive observation. Brooks 1975 articulates the triangulation logic for complex construction.

- **P5. Prefer offering help** [judgment, motivation] — Default to more assistance under uncertainty about readiness. Asymmetric error costs: under-helping creates failure that erodes both substrate AND belief about capability; over-helping has smaller costs on both dimensions and primarily costs efficiency. Whether the load-bearing mechanism is cognitive substrate, motivational belief, or both, the asymmetric default points the same direction (Tetzlaff 2025).
  - **P5a. Route help through external attribution when direct help threatens self-construct** [judgment, motivation] — Operationalizes T9b, T10. Sierksma & Brummelman 2025 (3 preregistered experiments, N=619, ages 7-9): both direct help and indirect help reduce children's competence feelings; direct help carries additional negative effects. When direct teacher-to-learner help would threaten the learner's self-construct, route through external source (book, video, third party) to preserve the help while reducing competence-cost. Not a categorical preference: both have motivational costs; direct help is sometimes right (when requested, when no external source available, when relational cost outweighs competence cost).
  - **P5b. Develop the learner's help-seeking metacognition** [judgment, motivation] — Operationalizes T9b, T15. Knowing when to ask for help vs persist independently is itself a learnable skill (Nelson-Le Gall 1981; Newman 1990; Karabenick 2003). Motivational cost of help is reduced when learner has initiated it. Teacher's role: respect refusal while signaling availability; make asking low-cost (no shame); model own help-seeking; gradually increase threshold for offering unsolicited help. Goal: teacher intervention frequency decreases over time as learner-initiated help-seeking develops.

- **P6. Embedded life as primary teaching aid** [planning] — Embedding skill encounters in the learner's actual life is the most powerful teaching aid available. Full immersion provides dense varied inputs that build language (T7), vocabulary (T5), and opportunities for natural use (T6). Embedded learning is not unguided learning: the teacher is only teaching if actively monitoring ambient encounters and raising them to active engagement when aligned with teaching goals. Isolated instruction produces brittle representations (T3) — book smart without field competence. Educational systems default to isolated assessment because it scales; teacher-mediated contexts don't have that constraint. Skills life supplies through ambient encounter get away with less deliberate practice; when ambient encounters dry up as life specializes, apparent regression often reflects environmental change, not learner change.

- **P7. Sequencing acquisition then variation** [planning] — Blocked practice during acquisition (stage 2): repeat the same form to allow novice schema-building without overload. Interleaved practice after acquisition (stage 3+): reintroduce variation requiring reconstruction across instances. Stage is the condition that determines which prescription applies. Joins cognitive-load tradition (acquisition) with desirable-difficulties tradition (post-acquisition).

- **P8. Honest purpose** [motivation] — Provide the why through honest information in the learner's current language (SMART from learner's vocabulary; goals require substrate to parse, T10). Acceptable honest answers: practical use, prerequisite for later work, credentialing requirement. Visible distal goals combined with calibrated proximal engagement accelerate learning (Locke and Latham). Translation from framework vocabulary to learner vocabulary is the parent's responsibility, not the recommender's — one structural reason the architecture is parent-mediated.
  - **P8a. Manufactured whys have costs** [motivation] — If you must manufacture a why for the student because they won't engage, know it has a cost. Teachers can install whys (threats, bribes, comparisons, manufactured significance). Some learning beats no learning, so deliberate deployment when nothing else produces engagement is acceptable; but using these tools when the learner is already engaged is worse than having no why at all. Empirical backing: Linnenbrink-Garcia 2008, Richardson 2012, Van Yperen 2014.
  - **P8b. Prompt the learner to affirm their own why when it's under pressure** [motivation] — Operationalizes T9. Prompting learner self-affirmation is a high-leverage motivation intervention; effects are substantially larger than belief-installation interventions covered in T9c (self-affirmation d = 0.41 across 144 studies, Escobar-Soler 2024, vs growth mindset d = 0.03-0.10, grit r = 0.18, self-efficacy β = 0.071). Structural distinction: belief installation tries to update beliefs from outside; self-affirmation has the learner generate their own articulation. Effects moderated by identity threat presence. Teacher-manufactured affirmation is less effective than learner-initiated. The teacher's role is creating the prompt, not providing the content.

- **P9. Binning as decision-support tool** [judgment] — Bin freely as engineering commitment for action; don't claim cognitive truth. Cognition is continuous, but action requires categorical decisions, so categorical descriptions of state are needed. Framework's 5-stage system (not-ready-to-learn, gap-aware, can-perform-with-conscious-thought, forgot-how-they-figured-it-out [T15-grounded], deliberately-reconstructed-what-stage-4-forgot [T14-grounded]) was built to mediate between cognitive science and intuition during framework construction. Practitioners may find it unworkable and adopt alternative binning systems (Multiple Intelligences, ZPD, mastery binary) whichever better supports their decision-making. Substantive treatment of binning systems lives in the binning paper.

- **P10. Trait input as starting prior** [judgment] — Trait measurements (Big Five or similar) compress biological substrate plus accumulated experience plus environmental supports into a snapshot usable as starting prior for activity recommendations. The compression is lossy; the framework cannot disentangle the three factors. Framework predictions are confined to traits with strong meta-analytic support for academic outcomes: conscientiousness (strongest predictor; Mammadov 2022; Chen et al. 2025) and openness (particularly at elementary/middle school level). Effects on academic performance are stronger in childhood than later education levels. Rank-order stability increases from r = 0.31 in childhood to plateau at 0.74 in mid-adulthood (Roberts & DelVecchio 2000). The framework makes no predictions for extraversion, agreeableness, or neuroticism given small or null associations in current meta-analyses.

- **P11. Composite activities produce integration schemas distinct from component schemas** [planning] — A composite activity exercises multiple skills together and produces integrated capability that none of the components produce alone. Aligns with Cognitive Flexibility Theory (Spiro et al. 1988): advanced knowledge in complex domains requires multiple alternative linkages among knowledge elements and schema assembly rather than retrieval of prepackaged schemas. Composite activities are by definition high-element-interactivity (Pyke 2024); T10's substrate-gating handles the cognitive load consideration.

- **P12. Full-execution scaffolding for procedure-level substrate gaps** [execution] — Mentor walks the activity end-to-end while the learner engages atomic parts only. Empirical grounding has two components: worked examples effect (Crissman 2006: d = 0.55-0.73 across domains; foundational Sweller & Cooper 1985) and Rogoff's guided participation (1990), which describes the collaborative process where the more skilled partner handles parts beyond the learner's current capability while the learner participates at parts they can perform — documented across cultural contexts including Mayan mothers weaving with children, caregiving, and motor skill acquisition. P12 operationalizes guided participation for the specific case where procedure-level substrate is the gap. Handover follows fading guidance pattern. Scope requires expert to have both fluent integrated execution (T15) and articulated decomposition for teaching (T14 / PCK / Shulman). Medical observational learning literature (Custers 1999) and cognitive apprenticeship (Collins, Brown & Newman 1989) describe related models.

- **P13. Spaced review with active recall for non-ambient skills** [planning] — Spacing is a universal requirement for retention; ambient encounter (P6) satisfies it automatically when available; scheduled review with active recall is the prescription when life isn't supplying the spacing. Classification of skills as ambient vs explicit determines whether spaced-repetition prescription applies. Effect sizes vary by domain and setting; recent meta-analyses in math (Murray 2025: g = 0.18) and online learning (Sigayret 2026: failure to replicate) suggest smaller effects than the original lab tradition reported.
- **P15. Practicing past mastery has sharp diminishing returns** [planning] — Practice past functional usability produces automation (T15) but with sharply diminishing learning gains per unit of practice (Pyke 2024 ceiling effect). For most skills, mastery is the appropriate stop-condition — time better spent on downstream skills or independent study. Exception: substrate skills (basic arithmetic for problem solving, letter recognition for reading, scales for music) warrant continued practice because automation frees cognitive resources for higher-level work.
- **P16. Build motivation through mastery experiences** [motivation] — Operationalizes T9b, T9c. Belief-based motivation interventions (growth mindset, grit training, self-efficacy work) produce real but modest effects. Mastery experience — actual performance success — is the strongest single source of self-efficacy in the literature (Britner & Pajares 2006; Usher & Pajares 2008). Build motivation through enabling performance success rather than through belief installation.
- **P17. Recognize burnout signs and respond with workload reduction plus recovery support** [motivation] — Operationalizes T16. Burnout protective factors (Sukamto 2026): sustainable workload, adaptive learning and coping strategies, autonomous motivation, social support, resilience-supportive environment. Type of social support matters (Messina 2025): venting paradoxically exacerbates burnout; reassurance-seeking helps. Recognize early signs (declining engagement despite continued effort, cynicism, sense of inability) and respond with reduced workload plus recovery support structured around reassurance about capability and temporary nature of challenge rather than open-ended venting (mindfulness, CBT, or REBT per T16).

## Stage binning (under P9)

- **Stage 1**: Not ready to learn (residual category). Substrate absent for the target activity, motivational void, social-cultural defense, or clinical conditions. Teacher's job is diagnostic — figure out why instruction isn't working.

- **Stage 2**: Gap-aware. Learner can use evidence to update self-model; recognizes they don't know something. Blocked practice reduces element interactivity for novice schema-building.

- **Stage 3**: Can perform with conscious thought. Substrate built; learner produces correct outputs but with effort and attention. Interleaved practice introduces variation that requires reconstruction.

- **Stage 4**: Forgot how they figured it out. Procedural integration complete (Fitts-Posner autonomous phase, ACT-R compilation). Conscious access to the steps is no longer available; instruction at the procedure level no longer needed; maintenance against decay (T6) is the dominant concern.

- **Stage 5**: Deliberately reconstructed for transmission. Practitioner has decomposed what stage 4 forgot, for teaching purposes. Operationalizes T14 (PCK as distinct knowledge type) within a developmental sequence. Framework's specific addition to Broadwell's four-stage tradition.
